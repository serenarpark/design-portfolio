Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

As someone who is not well versed in the design principles that we have been learning, it is interesting to see the rhetoric used. We have learned a lot about not only advocating for the clients and being able to create their vision, but also advocating for ourselves as the designer. I think this is positive framing of the dynamic between the designer and the client, as I would typically think about mainly advocating for the one receiving the work. Technology is arguably one of the biggest components of designing. I had never used most of the applications we have learned to use in this class, which definitely included a learning curve. As I learned to use different features it became very helpful and more fun bringing ideas to life. I've learned that there is a website and a tool for everything someone can think of. It is also extremely important to be ethical in the process of designing considering all people, and being mindful of how other work is referenced and utilized.

2. Leverage Design Principles

It was very interesting seeing design principles laid out in the reading with examples because I felt like a lot of the principles made a lot of sense visually, without having had prior knowledge about them. I had never thought very deeply about things like font, layout, colors, etc. However, I would often prefer the "proper" use of design principles as it visually made sense. I feel like I have a much better idea of what is aesthetically pleasing when piecing designs together, although I still need improvement.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

I had a lot of fun with the collaboration in this class. I enjoyed working with my table group and getting to know different people's interests and how they approached their designs. It was helpful in gaining inspiration, but also the feedback was always really useful. I definitely appreciated hearing others' opinions as I don't have a very design oriented mind, and often took the adivce of others.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

There was definitely a lot of revision neccesary in the design process for every project. Even when I would be proud of how something would look, after hearing feedback I would realize there were a lot of elements in my designs that need improvement. It was helpful in improving my skill and my eye. The casual nature of the class involving bouncing back ideas made even the improvement process enjoyable.

5. Learn to present your work.

I enjoyed presenting our work in small groups with people I was comfortable being vulnerable with; it took off pressure. It was also nice being able to get additional feedback with the option to showcase our designs to the class. It was interesting learning how to choose which elements of our design we wanted to explain and point out, and I think I've improved at specifically presenting design work.
